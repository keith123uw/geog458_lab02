## Narrative: Comparing YouTube Discourse on “War,” “Middle East,” and “Iran”

### Topic and Search Parameters
This project analyzes how different topics are represented on YouTube using word clouds. Data was collected from these three search queries: **“war,” “middle east,” and “iran.”**

### Purpose of Comparison
This is a massive topic of concern at this time and there are big key terms that are associated with it.

### Comparison of Word Clouds

- **War:** Broad and general. Words like *war, president, trump, news, military* show a mix of global conflicts and media coverage.
- **Middle East:** More regional. Includes *iran, israel, us, war*, showing connections between countries and ongoing conflicts.
- **Iran:** Most specific. Focused on *iran, trump, attack, nuclear*, highlighting political tension and U.S.-Iran relations.

### Possible Reasons

- Broader terms bring more diverse content
- Specific terms lead to more repeated keywords
- YouTube content reflects current events and politics

### Improvements

- Use more data (descriptions, comments, transcripts)
- Compare across platforms
- Filter by time period or video length?

### Observations

- Political figures (like Trump) appear in all searches
- Specific searches produce more focused language
- Search terms strongly shape the narrative of results
